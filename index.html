My Projects

Explore my engineering projects that demonstrate my skills in robotics, thermal systems, and engineering analysis.

Precision Robotic Arm

A compact robotic arm designed to promote recycling by picking up crumpled paper and placing it into a miniature dumpster.

The Precision Robotic Arm is a low-cost, Arduino Nano-powered system designed to automate paper waste handling with accuracy and efficiency. Built on a sturdy multilayered wood base, it incorporates five SG90 micro servos that enable base rotation, arm articulation, and precise gripper control for grasping and releasing paper.

A 12V battery powers the system, ensuring smooth servo operation, while all components are neatly housed on-board. Completed for just $25, this project demonstrates an affordable and practical approach to automation in sustainable waste management.

Thermal Sensor Safe

A secure safe locking mechanism using temperature sensor and servo motor technology.

The Thermal Sensor Safe is a secure locking system that integrates a temperature sensor and servo motor to enable innovative, sensor-based authentication. Designed in SOLIDWORKS for precise 3D printing and interlocking assembly, the mechanism was collaboratively developed to ensure smooth operation and reliable security.

This project highlights the fusion of electronic sensing with mechanical design to create a functional and intelligent access control solution.

SOLIDWORKS 3D Printing Servo Motors Temperature Sensors

MSATool: Matrix Structural Analysis

Research on modeling nonlinear systems and estimating their response rates across given limits.

This research project addresses the challenges of modeling complex engineering systems as nonlinear structures and estimating the rate at which their responses exceed defined thresholds with a positive slope—an effort that involves both theoretical and computational complexity.

The work focuses on developing accurate models for the spectral and probabilistic behavior of parameters and excitations, with applications including corroding pipelines under internal pressure fluctuations and shear buildings modeled as nonlinear oscillators.

As part of the research team, I created a MATLAB-based computational tool that performs matrix structural analysis to evaluate the probability of failure for various systems under different loading conditions. This project was supported by a $2,000 UROP (Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program) grant, which enabled the development and validation of this tool for practical use in reliability analysis.
